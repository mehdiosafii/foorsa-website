Scholarship Types

It is impossible to list every scholarship available in China due to the numerous universities and organizations that offer them.

However, here are some of the most common scholarships for international students in China:

Moroccan students can access multiple scholarship types to study in China, with funding ranging from partial to full coverage. The most prestigious is the Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC), which covers tuition, accommodation, monthly stipend (3,000-3,500 RMB), and health insurance. University scholarships are offered by individual institutions and typically cover 50-100% of tuition fees. Provincial government scholarships from cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangdong provide regional funding opportunities. Confucius Institute scholarships target Chinese language learners, while Belt and Road Initiative scholarships support students from participating countries including Morocco. Foorsa helps Moroccan students identify suitable scholarships, prepare strong applications, and navigate the Chinese admission system. Applications typically open 6-8 months before semester start, with March and September intakes available. Updated on: 2026-02-23

Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC Scholarship)

This scholarship, offered by the Chinese government, is the most prestigious, encompassing tuition fees, accommodation, a living stipend, and health insurance.

Local Government Scholarships

These scholarships are offered by provincial and municipal governments in China. Although they are typically less competitive than CSC scholarships, they may have specific eligibility requirements, such as being from a certain country or region.

University Scholarships

Many universities in China offer their own scholarships to international students, which can vary in value and coverage of expenses.

Confucius Institute Scholarships

These scholarships are offered by Confucius Institutes around the world to students who want to study Chinese language and culture in China.

Silk Road Scholarships

These scholarships are offered by the Chinese government to students from countries involved in the Belt and Road Initiative.

Spring Bud Project

This program offers scholarships to orphans and students from low-income families in China.

Here are some additional tips for finding scholarships to study in China:

  • Apply to Foorsa early, as application deadlines for many scholarships are several months before the start of the academic year.
  • Follow the Foorsa instructions carefully.
  • Submit a strong application package that includes a well-written essay, letters of recommendation, and transcripts.

How Much Does a CSC Scholarship Cover for Moroccan Students?

The Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC) provides comprehensive financial support including full tuition waiver, on-campus accommodation (or housing allowance), monthly living stipend of 3,000-3,500 RMB (approximately $420-490 USD), comprehensive medical insurance, and one-time settlement allowance. This makes it the most generous option for Moroccan students pursuing undergraduate, master's, or doctoral degrees in China.

What Are the Eligibility Requirements for China Scholarships?

General eligibility for Chinese scholarships requires: Moroccan citizenship or valid passport, strong academic record (minimum 70-80% average for undergraduate, 75-85% for postgraduate), age limits (typically under 25 for bachelor's, under 35 for master's, under 40 for PhD), language proficiency (HSK 4 for Chinese-taught programs or IELTS 6.0+ for English-taught), good health certificate, and clean criminal record. Each scholarship type has specific additional requirements.

How Competitive Are Chinese Scholarships for Moroccan Students?

Competition varies by scholarship type. CSC scholarships have acceptance rates of 15-25% depending on the field of study (STEM fields typically have higher acceptance). University scholarships are moderately competitive (30-40% acceptance), while provincial scholarships vary widely. Moroccan students benefit from Morocco's strong diplomatic relationship with China and growing educational partnerships. Fields like Engineering, Medicine, Business, and Computer Science attract the most applicants, while Chinese Language and Culture programs have higher acceptance rates.

Can I Get a Partial Scholarship If I Don't Qualify for Full Funding?

Yes! Many universities offer partial scholarships covering 25-75% of tuition fees. These are easier to obtain than full scholarships and can be combined with self-funding for living expenses. Some universities also offer performance-based scholarships that can be upgraded after your first year of strong academic performance.
