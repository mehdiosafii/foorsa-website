Frequently Asked Questions

What are the requirements to study Architecture in China?

To study Architecture in China, you typically need a high school diploma (or equivalent) with good grades, a valid passport, a health certificate, and proof of English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL) or Chinese proficiency (HSK) depending on the language of instruction. Foorsa can guide you through the entire application process.

How much does it cost to study Architecture in China?

Tuition fees for Architecture programs in China range from $2,000 to $8,000 per year depending on the university. Living costs in China are affordable, typically $300-$600 per month. Many universities offer scholarships covering tuition and living expenses.

Are there scholarships available for Architecture in China?

Yes, numerous scholarships are available including the Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC), Confucius Institute Scholarship, provincial scholarships, and university-specific scholarships. Foorsa helps Moroccan students apply for these scholarships to study Architecture in China.

What career opportunities are available after studying Architecture?

Graduates in Architecture can pursue diverse careers both in Morocco and internationally. The Visual & Performance Arts sector offers growing opportunities, and a degree from a Chinese university is increasingly valued by employers worldwide.

Is Architecture taught in English in Chinese universities?

Yes, many top Chinese universities offer Architecture programs fully taught in English. Some programs are bilingual (English and Chinese). Foorsa can help you find English-taught Architecture programs that match your profile.
