September vs March Intake China 2026: Complete Guide for Moroccan Students

Published on February 24, 2026 | By Foorsa Team

Chinese universities offer two main intakes annually: September (fall semester, primary intake) and March (spring semester, secondary intake). September intake is the major admission period with full scholarship availability including CSC, wider program selection, and application deadlines from January to May. March intake has limited program options, fewer scholarships, and deadlines from September to December. For most Moroccan students, September intake is recommended due to better scholarship opportunities and alignment with Morocco's academic calendar ending in June. Foorsa is Morocco's leading agency specializing in Chinese university admissions and scholarships, providing expert guidance on intake selection, application timing, and comprehensive support to maximize your admission success.

Understanding Chinese University Intake System

Unlike Morocco's single annual university entry, Chinese universities operate on a semester system with two distinct admission periods:

Intake Period Also Known As Semester Start Application Period Primary Features
September Intake Fall Intake, Autumn Intake Early-Mid September January - May (varies by university) Primary admission period, all programs available, full scholarship options
March Intake Spring Intake, Winter Intake Early-Mid March September - December (previous year) Secondary admission period, limited programs, fewer scholarships

Academic Calendar Structure:

  • Fall Semester: September to January (includes winter vacation in January)
  • Spring Semester: March to July (includes summer vacation July-August)

September Intake: The Primary Choice

Why September is Recommended for Moroccan Students

✅ Advantages of September Intake:

  • Maximum scholarship availability: CSC Scholarship exclusively for September, plus university and provincial scholarships
  • Full program selection: All majors, specializations, and degree levels available
  • Perfect timing for Moroccan graduates: Baccalauréat results in June → Apply immediately → Start in September
  • Larger international student cohort: More networking opportunities and social activities
  • Better weather transition: Arrive in mild autumn weather (15-25°C) vs harsh winter (March)
  • Complete academic year experience: Start fresh at beginning of university calendar
  • More competitive but fair: Higher applicant pool but proportionally more spots

❌ Disadvantages of September Intake:

  • More competitive due to higher number of applicants globally
  • Earlier application deadlines require planning ahead (December-January prep)
  • Peak season for visa processing (may have slight delays in July-August)

March Intake: The Alternative Option

When to Consider March Intake

March intake works well if you:

  • ✅ Missed September deadline or received late admission results
  • ✅ Want to start studies immediately without waiting 6+ months
  • ✅ Are flexible with program choices (willing to adapt to limited availability)
  • ✅ Can afford self-funded tuition (scholarships very limited)
  • ✅ Graduated mid-year or have gap year flexibility

✅ Advantages of March Intake:

  • Faster start: Don't wait until September if you're ready to go
  • Less competition: Fewer applicants, potentially easier admission for qualified candidates
  • Second chance opportunity: If you were rejected for September, reapply with improved profile
  • Smaller class sizes: More personalized attention from professors
  • Unique graduation timing: Graduate in January/February (off-peak job market entry)

❌ Disadvantages of March Intake:

  • Very limited CSC scholarships: Most CSC funding reserved for September
  • Reduced program availability: Science labs, clinical rotations, some engineering programs unavailable
  • Harsh winter arrival: March weather in northern China can be cold (-5 to 10°C)
  • Smaller international student community: Fewer orientation activities and networking events
  • Awkward timing for Moroccan students: Applying September-December interrupts final Baccalauréat year

Scholarship Availability by Intake

Scholarship Type September Intake March Intake
CSC Scholarship ✅ Fully available (primary cycle) ❌ Not available (rare exceptions)
University Scholarships ✅ Full availability (50-100% tuition) ⚠️ Limited (30-50% tuition, fewer slots)
Provincial Government Scholarships ✅ Available ⚠️ Very limited
Confucius Institute Scholarships ✅ Available (language programs mainly) ⚠️ Limited (short-term programs only)
Self-Funded (No Scholarship) ✅ Always available ✅ Always available

Critical Note: If you need a scholarship to afford studying in China, September intake is essentially your only option. CSC Scholarship covers 100% tuition, accommodation, living stipend, and insurance—making it the most valuable funding source for Moroccan students.

Program Availability Comparison

Programs Usually Available in Both Intakes:

  • ✅ Chinese Language Programs
  • ✅ Business Administration (MBA, Bachelor's)
  • ✅ International Relations
  • ✅ Economics and Finance
  • ✅ Computer Science (limited spots March)
  • ✅ English-taught Engineering (limited specializations March)

Programs Rarely/Never Available in March Intake:

  • ❌ Clinical Medicine (MBBS) - Hospital rotations schedule conflict
  • ❌ Laboratory Sciences (Chemistry, Biology, Physics) - Equipment/lab coordination issues
  • ❌ Architecture - Project-based curriculum requires fall start
  • ❌ Art and Design (some schools) - Portfolio review cycle tied to September
  • ❌ Specialized Engineering (Aerospace, Nuclear, Biomedical)

Decision Framework: Which Intake Should You Choose?

Choose September Intake If:

  • ✅ You need scholarship funding (especially CSC)
  • ✅ You're graduating Baccalauréat in June 2026
  • ✅ Your desired program is science/engineering/medicine
  • ✅ You want maximum university and program options
  • ✅ You prefer starting with a large international student cohort
  • ✅ You can plan and prepare 6-9 months in advance

Choose March Intake If:

  • ✅ You missed September 2025 deadline and don't want to wait until September 2026
  • ✅ You're self-funded and don't need scholarships
  • ✅ Your program is available for March (Business, Chinese Language, Social Sciences)
  • ✅ You graduated mid-year or took a gap semester
  • ✅ You want to start immediately and are ready to go
  • ✅ You prefer smaller classes and more intimate learning environment

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Assuming all programs have March intake: Always verify with university before assuming availability
  • ❌ Missing September deadline thinking "I'll just do March": Many programs don't offer March option
  • ❌ Applying to March without checking scholarship options: Discover too late that no funding is available
  • ❌ Starting preparation too late: Both intakes require 2-3 months document preparation
  • ❌ Choosing March solely to "start faster": Waiting for September with scholarship beats starting March self-funded
  • ❌ Not considering weather: Northern China in March can be harsh for students from warmer climates
  • ❌ Ignoring visa processing time: March intake has tighter timeline (Jan admission → March arrival = 6 weeks only)

Conclusion: Make the Right Choice for Your Future

For the vast majority of Moroccan students, September intake is the optimal choice. It offers maximum scholarships, full program availability, better timing with Morocco's academic calendar, and a complete university experience from day one.

March intake serves as a valuable alternative for specific situations—self-funded students, business/language majors, or those who missed September deadlines. However, don't rush into March intake without carefully considering the trade-offs.

Key Takeaway: Plan ahead. Starting your research and preparation in December for the following September gives you the best chance of admission success with full scholarship support.
