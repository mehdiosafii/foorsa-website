Study Medicine in China: Programs, Costs & Requirements

Published on February 22, 2026 | By Foorsa Team

China has emerged as one of the top destinations for international students pursuing medical degrees. With high-quality education, affordable tuition, English-taught programs, and globally recognized degrees, studying medicine (MBBS) in China offers Moroccan students an excellent pathway to becoming qualified doctors.

Why Study Medicine in China?

1. Globally Recognized Degrees

Chinese medical universities are recognized by major international bodies including:

  • World Health Organization (WHO)
  • Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG) - USA
  • Medical Council of India (MCI)
  • General Medical Council (GMC) - UK
  • Morocco Medical Council

Graduates can practice medicine in Morocco, Middle East, Africa, and many other countries after passing local licensing exams.

2. English-Taught Programs

Most medical universities in China offer full MBBS programs in English, eliminating the language barrier for international students.

3. Affordable Tuition

Compared to Western countries, studying medicine in China is significantly more affordable—often 50-70% cheaper than Europe or North America.

4. Advanced Facilities

Chinese medical universities feature state-of-the-art laboratories, modern teaching hospitals, and cutting-edge research facilities.

5. Clinical Training Opportunities

Students gain hands-on clinical experience in affiliated hospitals, working with real patients under supervision of experienced doctors.

MBBS Program Structure in China

The MBBS program in China typically lasts 6 years:

Years 1-2: Pre-Clinical Phase

  • Basic medical sciences (Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry)
  • Chinese language and culture courses
  • Foundation subjects

Years 3-5: Clinical Phase

  • Clinical medicine subjects (Pathology, Pharmacology, Microbiology)
  • Clinical rotations in teaching hospitals
  • Practical training in various departments

Year 6: Internship

  • Full-time clinical internship in affiliated hospitals
  • Hands-on patient care under supervision
  • Final examinations and thesis defense

Cost of Studying Medicine in China

Tuition Fees

  • Top-tier universities: $6,000 - $8,000 per year
  • Mid-tier universities: $4,500 - $6,000 per year
  • Total 6-year tuition: $27,000 - $48,000

Living Expenses

  • Accommodation: $600 - $1,500/year (on-campus dormitory)
  • Food: $150 - $250/month
  • Books & supplies: $300 - $500/year
  • Transportation: $20 - $50/month
  • Health insurance: $100 - $150/year (mandatory)
  • Total living cost: $2,500 - $4,000/year

Total 6-Year Investment

Tuition + Living: $42,000 - $72,000 for the entire MBBS degree

💡 Scholarship Tip: Many medical universities offer scholarships that cover 50-100% of tuition fees. The CSC Scholarship fully funds medical studies including accommodation and living stipend!

Admission Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma (Baccalauréat) with minimum 60% grades
  • Strong performance in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology
  • Age: 18-25 years old at the time of admission

English Proficiency

  • IELTS 5.5+ or TOEFL 65+ (some universities waive this for English-medium education background)
  • Interview may be conducted to assess English communication skills

Required Documents

  • Completed application form
  • Passport copy
  • High school diploma and transcripts (notarized & translated)
  • English proficiency certificate
  • Personal statement/study plan
  • Two recommendation letters
  • Physical examination form
  • No criminal record certificate
  • Passport-size photos

Application Process & Timeline

September Intake (Main Intake)

  • December - February: Research universities and prepare documents
  • March - June: Submit applications
  • June - July: Receive admission letters
  • July - August: Apply for X1 student visa

Application Tips

  • Apply to 2-3 universities to increase admission chances
  • Start application process 6-8 months before intake
  • Top universities fill spots quickly—apply early!
  • Ensure all documents are properly notarized and translated

Recognition & Licensing

Practicing in Morocco

After graduation, Moroccan students must:

  1. Have their degree verified by the Moroccan Ministry of Higher Education
  2. Pass the Moroccan medical licensing examination (if required)
  3. Complete any additional requirements by the Moroccan Medical Council

International Practice

  • USA: Pass USMLE (United States Medical Licensing Examination)
  • UK: Pass PLAB (Professional and Linguistic Assessments Board) test
  • Middle East: Most countries accept Chinese medical degrees with local licensing exams

Chinese medical degrees from accredited universities are widely recognized, making graduates eligible for licensing exams in most countries.

Common Questions

Do I need to learn Chinese?

No, English-medium MBBS programs don't require Chinese proficiency. However, learning basic Chinese helps with daily life and patient communication during clinical rotations. Most universities offer Chinese language courses.

Can I work while studying?

International students are generally not permitted to work off-campus during their studies. Focus should remain on academics and clinical training.

Is the degree valid in Morocco?

Yes, medical degrees from recognized Chinese universities are valid in Morocco. Graduates must follow the standard licensing process through the Moroccan medical authorities.
