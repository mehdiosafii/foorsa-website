Are Chinese Degrees Recognized in Morocco? Complete Guide 2026

Published on March 7, 2026 | By Foorsa Team

One of the most common questions Moroccan students ask before committing to studying in China is: "Will my Chinese degree be recognized back home?" The short answer is yes -- Chinese degrees from accredited universities are recognized in Morocco. However, the process involves specific steps, documentation, and timelines that every student should understand before departing.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about Chinese degree recognition in Morocco in 2026, including bilateral agreements, the ENIC-NARIC equivalence process, required documents, costs, and special cases for regulated professions like medicine and engineering.

Morocco-China Bilateral Education Agreements

Morocco and China have maintained strong diplomatic and educational ties for decades. Several key agreements underpin the recognition of Chinese academic credentials in Morocco:

Key Bilateral Frameworks

  • Cultural and Educational Cooperation Agreement (1995, renewed 2016): This foundational treaty establishes mutual recognition of academic qualifications between the two countries and provides a legal basis for degree equivalence.
  • Belt and Road Education Initiative: Since Morocco joined the Belt and Road Initiative in 2017, educational cooperation has expanded significantly, with more Moroccan students receiving scholarships to study in China.
  • CSC Scholarship Framework: The Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC) program, which funds hundreds of Moroccan students annually, operates under inter-governmental agreements that implicitly support degree recognition. Learn more about the CSC Scholarship 2026.
  • Confucius Institute Partnerships: Morocco hosts multiple Confucius Institutes, reflecting deep institutional ties that facilitate academic mobility and credential recognition.

These agreements mean that, in principle, Chinese degrees are accepted for equivalence evaluation in Morocco. However, having an agreement in place does not mean automatic recognition -- graduates must still go through the formal equivalence process.

The ENIC-NARIC Equivalence Process in Morocco

Morocco's degree equivalence system is managed by the Ministry of Higher Education, Scientific Research, and Innovation, which acts as the national ENIC-NARIC center. The process is known as obtaining an "attestation d'equivalence" (equivalence certificate).

What is ENIC-NARIC?

ENIC (European Network of Information Centres) and NARIC (National Academic Recognition Information Centres) form a global network that facilitates international academic credential recognition. Morocco participates in this network through its Ministry of Higher Education, which evaluates foreign degrees and determines their equivalence to Moroccan qualifications.

Types of Equivalence

The Moroccan system offers two main types of recognition:

  • Academic Equivalence (Equivalence Academique): Establishes the level of your degree within the Moroccan education framework (Licence, Master, Doctorat). This is used for further studies or public sector employment.
  • Professional Equivalence (Equivalence Professionnelle): Required for regulated professions (medicine, engineering, architecture, pharmacy). This involves additional evaluation by the relevant professional council or order.

Step-by-Step Equivalence Process

  1. Authenticate your Chinese degree: Before leaving China, have your degree authenticated by the Chinese Ministry of Education through the China Academic Degrees and Graduate Education Development Center (CDGDC) or the China Higher Education Student Information Center (CHSI).
  2. Legalize documents at the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs: All academic documents must be legalized (apostilled) by the Chinese MFA.
  3. Authenticate at the Moroccan Embassy in Beijing: Have your documents authenticated by the Moroccan diplomatic mission in China.
  4. Translate all documents: Obtain certified French or Arabic translations of your degree, transcripts, and all supporting documents from a sworn translator in Morocco.
  5. Submit your equivalence application: File your application at the Ministry of Higher Education in Rabat or through their online portal, including all required documents.
  6. Evaluation and commission review: Your application will be reviewed by an equivalence commission that assesses the curriculum, institution accreditation, and program duration.
  7. Receive your equivalence certificate: Upon approval, you receive the attestation d'equivalence that formally recognizes your Chinese degree within the Moroccan education system.

Documents Needed for Degree Equivalence

Preparing the correct documentation is critical for a smooth equivalence process. Missing or incorrect documents are the most common reason for delays.

Required Documents Checklist

  • Original diploma (degree certificate) and two certified copies
  • Official academic transcripts for all years of study
  • CDGDC or CHSI degree verification report (obtained in China)
  • Certified French or Arabic translation of all academic documents by a sworn translator
  • Copy of your passport (pages with personal information and Chinese visa/residence permit)
  • Copy of your Moroccan national ID card (CIN)
  • Completed equivalence application form (available from the Ministry website)
  • Two passport-size photographs
  • Copy of your Baccalaureat diploma (high school diploma)
  • Legalization stamps from Chinese MFA and Moroccan Embassy in China

For Master's and Doctoral Degrees, Also Include:

  • Thesis or dissertation abstract (translated)
  • Publication list (if applicable)
  • Previous degree equivalence certificate (if the prior degree was also obtained abroad)

Timeline and Costs

Expected Timeline

  • Step 1 -- Chinese degree authentication (CDGDC/CHSI): 2-4 weeks
  • Step 2 -- Legalization at Chinese MFA: 1-2 weeks
  • Step 3 -- Authentication at Moroccan Embassy: 1-2 weeks
  • Step 4 -- Certified translations in Morocco: 1-2 weeks
  • Step 5 -- Ministry evaluation: 3-6 months (standard), up to 12 months (complex cases)
  • Total estimated time: 4-8 months for standard cases

Cost Breakdown

  • CDGDC/CHSI verification: ~300 CNY (~400 MAD)
  • Chinese MFA legalization: Free or nominal fee
  • Moroccan Embassy authentication: ~200-400 MAD
  • Certified translations: 500-2,000 MAD per document (depending on length)
  • Equivalence application fee: 200-500 MAD
  • Notarization of copies: 100-300 MAD per document
  • Total estimated cost: 2,000-5,000 MAD

Special Cases: Regulated Professions

Certain professions in Morocco require additional steps beyond the standard academic equivalence. These are known as regulated professions and include medicine, engineering, pharmacy, architecture, and veterinary science.

Medicine and Dentistry

Medical degree recognition is the most complex case. Moroccan students who study medicine in China must follow these specific steps:

  1. Verify WHO listing: Ensure your Chinese medical university is listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS). Only degrees from listed institutions are eligible.
  2. Obtain academic equivalence: Apply for standard degree equivalence from the Ministry of Higher Education.
  3. Pass the national medical licensing exam: Moroccan authorities may require graduates to pass a licensing examination or complete an internship at a Moroccan hospital.
  4. Register with the Conseil de l'Ordre des Medecins: After obtaining equivalence and passing any required exams, register with the Moroccan Medical Council to practice.

The entire process for medical degrees can take 12-18 months. Explore more in our medicine category.

Engineering

Engineering degrees from Chinese universities are generally well-recognized, especially from institutions with ABET-equivalent accreditation or those in the Washington Accord network. Steps include:

  1. Standard academic equivalence from the Ministry
  2. Registration with the Ordre National des Ingenieurs du Maroc (if practicing as a licensed engineer)
  3. Some employers may require additional technical assessments

Pharmacy

Pharmacy degrees require equivalence from both the Ministry of Higher Education and validation by the Conseil National de l'Ordre des Pharmaciens. Graduates may need to complete a supervised internship in Morocco.

Architecture

Architecture graduates must have their degree evaluated by the Ordre National des Architectes in addition to obtaining academic equivalence.

Tips for a Smooth Recognition Process

  1. Start the process before leaving China: Obtain your CDGDC/CHSI verification, MFA legalization, and Moroccan Embassy authentication before returning to Morocco. This saves significant time.
  2. Keep multiple certified copies: Make at least three notarized copies of every document. You will need them for various applications.
  3. Choose a reputable sworn translator: Poor translations are a common reason for application rejection. Use a sworn translator (traducteur assermente) registered with the Moroccan courts.
  4. Maintain a complete academic portfolio: Keep all documents from your time in China -- student ID, course syllabi, internship certificates, research publications, and recommendation letters.
  5. Follow up regularly: After submitting your application, follow up with the Ministry every 4-6 weeks to check the status.
  6. Network with alumni: Connect with other Moroccan graduates from Chinese universities who have successfully completed the equivalence process. They can share practical advice and recommendations.

Common Challenges and How to Overcome Them

Challenge 1: Program Duration Differences

Chinese Bachelor's programs are typically 4 years, while some Moroccan equivalents may be 3 years (Licence) or 5 years (Diplome d'Ingenieur). The equivalence commission considers the total credit hours and curriculum content, not just duration. Providing detailed course descriptions and syllabi helps the commission make an accurate assessment.

Challenge 2: Language of Instruction Verification

If your program was taught in English or Chinese, you may need to provide a certificate from the university confirming the language of instruction. This is especially important for programs where Morocco has a strong French-language tradition.

Challenge 3: Online or Distance Learning Components

Programs with significant online components may face additional scrutiny. If your program included online coursework (common during the post-COVID era), obtain a letter from your university clarifying the in-person vs. online ratio.

Challenge 4: Degree Naming Conventions

Chinese degree titles may not directly correspond to Moroccan equivalents. For example, a Chinese "Bachelor of Engineering" may be evaluated differently from a Moroccan "Diplome d'Ingenieur d'Etat." The equivalence commission will review the full curriculum to determine the appropriate Moroccan equivalent.

Recognition for Employment and Further Studies

Public Sector Employment

To work in the Moroccan public sector (fonction publique), you must have your degree equivalence before applying. Government positions strictly require the attestation d'equivalence. Start the process early if you plan to take concours (public sector exams).

Private Sector Employment

Many private Moroccan employers and multinational companies accept Chinese degrees without formal equivalence, especially from well-known universities. However, having the official equivalence certificate strengthens your position and may be required for certain roles.

Further Studies in Morocco

If you wish to pursue further studies at a Moroccan university (e.g., a Master's after a Chinese Bachelor's), you will need degree equivalence to enroll. Some Moroccan universities may offer provisional admission while your equivalence is being processed.

International Recognition

Beyond Morocco, Chinese degrees are widely recognized internationally. If you plan to work in Europe, the Middle East, or other regions, the degree verification from CDGDC/CHSI serves as a global authentication. Many countries have their own equivalence processes similar to Morocco's.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Chinese degrees recognized in Morocco?

Yes, Chinese degrees from universities accredited by the Chinese Ministry of Education are recognized in Morocco through the formal equivalence process. Morocco and China have bilateral education agreements that facilitate this recognition. You must apply for an attestation d'equivalence through the Moroccan Ministry of Higher Education.

How long does the equivalence process take?

The typical timeline is 3-6 months from the date of submitting a complete application. Complex cases involving regulated professions (medicine, engineering) may take up to 12 months. Starting the document preparation process while still in China can significantly reduce the total time.

What if my university is not well-known in Morocco?

As long as your university is accredited by the Chinese Ministry of Education and listed in their official directory, your degree is eligible for equivalence evaluation. The commission evaluates the institution, curriculum, and program structure rather than relying solely on name recognition.

Can I work in Morocco while waiting for equivalence?

Yes, you can work in the private sector while your equivalence is being processed. However, public sector positions and regulated professions require the equivalence certificate before you can start working. Some private employers may offer conditional employment pending your equivalence.

Is a Chinese medical degree valid for practicing medicine in Morocco?

Chinese medical degrees can be recognized, but the process is more rigorous. Your university must be WHO-listed, you must obtain academic equivalence, and you may need to pass a Moroccan medical licensing exam and register with the Conseil de l'Ordre des Medecins. Read our detailed guide on studying medicine in China.

Do I need to translate my documents into French or Arabic?

Yes, all documents must be translated into French or Arabic by a sworn translator (traducteur assermente) registered with the Moroccan courts. French translations are more commonly used for the equivalence process.
