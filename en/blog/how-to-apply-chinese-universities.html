How to Apply for Chinese Universities: Step-by-Step Guide 2026

Published on February 22, 2026 | By Foorsa Team

Applying to Chinese universities can seem daunting, especially for Moroccan students unfamiliar with the Chinese education system. However, with the right guidance and preparation, the process becomes straightforward. This comprehensive guide will walk you through every step of applying to Chinese universities in 2026.

Understanding the Chinese University Application System

Unlike centralized application systems in some countries, China has a decentralized approach. This means you apply directly to each university through their respective application portals. Most top Chinese universities accept international students through three main pathways:

  • Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC) - Fully funded scholarships managed by the China Scholarship Council
  • University Scholarships - Individual university-funded scholarships
  • Self-Funded Programs - Pay tuition fees yourself

For Moroccan students, the CSC scholarship route is the most popular due to its comprehensive coverage. Learn more about scholarship opportunities available to you.

Step 1: Research and Choose Your Program

Identify Your Field of Study

China offers programs in virtually every field imaginable. Popular choices among Moroccan students include:

  • Medicine (MBBS) - taught in English at many universities
  • Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, Computer Science)
  • Business Administration and Economics
  • Chinese Language and Literature
  • International Relations and Political Science
  • Architecture and Urban Planning

Explore all available majors and programs to find the perfect fit for your career goals.

Select Target Universities

China has over 2,000 universities, but focus on institutions that:

  • Are listed in the top 500-1000 globally (QS, Times Higher Education rankings)
  • Offer programs in English (if you don't speak Chinese)
  • Are recognized by Morocco's Ministry of Higher Education
  • Have strong programs in your chosen field
  • Offer scholarships to international students

💡 Pro Tip: Apply to 3-5 universities: one top-tier (highly competitive), 2-3 mid-tier universities, and one safety option. This maximizes your chances of acceptance.

Step 2: Check Eligibility Requirements

Academic Requirements

Different degree levels have different requirements:

Bachelor's Degree Programs:

  • High school diploma (Baccalauréat) with minimum 12/20 average
  • Age: Usually under 25 years old
  • Good health condition
  • No criminal record

Master's Degree Programs:

  • Bachelor's degree with minimum GPA of 3.0/4.0 or equivalent (14/20)
  • Age: Usually under 35 years old
  • Relevant academic background to your chosen master's program
  • Two recommendation letters from professors

PhD Programs:

  • Master's degree with strong academic record
  • Age: Usually under 40 years old
  • Research proposal (1500-2000 words)
  • Publications or research experience (preferred)
  • Two recommendation letters from academic supervisors

Language Requirements

Most programs for international students are offered in English or Chinese. Requirements vary:

  • English-taught programs: IELTS 6.0+ or TOEFL 80+ (some universities waive this for students from English-speaking countries or those with English-medium education)
  • Chinese-taught programs: HSK 4 for undergraduate, HSK 5 for postgraduate programs
  • No language certificate? Many universities offer a one-year Chinese language preparatory program before your degree starts

Step 3: Prepare Required Documents

Document preparation is crucial. Here's a comprehensive checklist of what you'll need:

Mandatory Documents for All Applicants

  1. Passport Copy: Valid for at least 2 years from application date, all pages scanned
  2. Passport-sized Photos: Recent photos with white background (usually 4-6 copies)
  3. Highest Diploma: Notarized and translated to English or Chinese by an authorized translator
  4. Academic Transcripts: Complete transcripts from all previous institutions, notarized and translated
  5. Physical Examination Form: Completed at a designated clinic (Chinese Embassy provides list), valid for 6 months
  6. No Criminal Record Certificate: Obtained from local police station, notarized and translated
  7. Study Plan/Personal Statement: 800-1000 words explaining why you want to study in China and your academic goals
  8. Financial Guarantee: Bank statement (for self-funded students) or scholarship proof

Additional Documents for Postgraduate Programs

  • Two Recommendation Letters: From professors or academic supervisors, on official letterhead
  • Research Proposal: For PhD applicants (1500-2000 words)
  • Publications List: If you have published research papers
  • Work Experience Certificate: If applicable, especially for MBA programs

Need detailed guidance on document preparation? Check our comprehensive documents guide for step-by-step instructions and templates.

⚠️ Important: All documents must be notarized and translated by certified translators. Keep both original and translated copies. Universities require scanned copies for online applications, but you'll need originals when you arrive in China.

Step 4: Submit Online Applications

Application Portals

You'll need to apply through two main portals:

1. CSC Online Application System (for scholarship applicants)
Website: studyinchina.csc.edu.cn

  • Create an account using your email
  • Fill in personal information
  • Choose your program and university (up to 3 choices)
  • Upload required documents
  • Submit before deadline (usually April 30)
  • Print and keep your CSC application number

2. University Application Portal
Each university has its own online application system. You must apply to BOTH the CSC portal AND the university portal.

  • Visit the university's international students admission website
  • Create an account
  • Fill in the application form carefully
  • Upload all required documents (PDF format, usually under 2MB each)
  • Enter your CSC application number (if applying for scholarship)
  • Submit and pay application fee (if required, usually $50-150 for self-funded students)

Common Application Mistakes to Avoid

  • Submitting incomplete applications
  • Missing university-specific deadlines (some close in March)
  • Using low-quality scanned documents
  • Writing generic study plans that don't mention the specific university
  • Forgetting to apply to BOTH CSC and university portals
  • Uploading documents in wrong format or size

Step 5: Track Your Application

After submission, you can track your application status online:

  • Log in to the university portal regularly to check for updates
  • Check your email frequently (including spam folder) for communication from universities
  • Universities may request additional documents or interviews
  • Response time varies: 4-8 weeks for universities, CSC results come in June-July

Step 6: Receive Admission and Prepare for Departure

Admission Documents You'll Receive

  • Admission Letter (录取通知书): Official acceptance from the university
  • JW202/JW201 Form: Required for student visa application (for scholarship students)
  • Visa Application Form (Form V): To be completed for Chinese Embassy
  • Physical Examination Record: Instructions for health checks

Visa Application Process

Once you receive your admission documents, apply for an X1 student visa at the Chinese Embassy in Rabat:

  1. Complete Visa Application Form (Form V)
  2. Submit passport (valid for at least 6 months)
  3. Provide Admission Letter and JW202/JW201 form
  4. Submit physical examination record
  5. Provide passport photos
  6. Pay visa fee (approximately 300 MAD)
  7. Processing time: 4-7 working days

Tips for a Successful Application

1. Start Early

Begin your research and document preparation at least 6 months before the deadline. Early applications often have better chances of acceptance as universities have more available spots.

2. Tailor Your Study Plan

Don't use the same study plan for all universities. Customize each one to mention specific professors, research centers, or programs at that particular university. Show genuine interest and research.

3. Get Strong Recommendations

Choose professors who know your work well and can write detailed, personalized letters. Generic recommendation letters hurt your chances.

4. Proofread Everything

Grammar and spelling errors create a bad impression. Have someone fluent in English review your study plan and personal statement.

5. Contact the University

Email the international admissions office with questions. Showing initiative and genuine interest can make your application stand out. Some universities even offer pre-admission which significantly increases scholarship chances.

6. Consider Professional Help

Costs Involved in the Application Process

  • Application fees: $0-150 per university (often waived for scholarship applicants)
  • Document notarization and translation: 1,000-2,000 MAD
  • Physical examination: 500-800 MAD
  • Visa fee: approximately 300 MAD
  • Flight ticket to China: 6,000-12,000 MAD (one-way)
  • Initial living expenses: 3,000-5,000 MAD for first month
