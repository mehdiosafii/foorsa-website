Study in China for Free: All Scholarships Available in 2026

Published on March 7, 2026 | By Foorsa Team

Did you know that you can study in China COMPLETELY FREE? Every year, the Chinese government and universities offer thousands of scholarships covering 100% of tuition fees, accommodation, and even a monthly living allowance.

This comprehensive guide lists ALL scholarships available for Moroccan students in 2026, how to apply, and how to maximize your chances of getting one.

1. Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)

The most prestigious and comprehensive scholarship for studying in China.

What It Covers (Type A - Full Scholarship)

  • 100% tuition fees -- fully covered
  • Free accommodation (university dormitory)
  • Monthly living allowance:
    • Bachelor's/Master's: 2,500 RMB/month (~$360 USD)
    • PhD: 3,500 RMB/month (~$500 USD)
  • Comprehensive medical insurance
  • 1 year of preparatory Chinese language (if needed)

Your only cost: Personal expenses (~$150-250 USD/month for food and leisure)

Who Can Apply?

  • Bachelor's: High school diploma holder, under 25 years old
  • Master's: Bachelor's degree holder, under 35 years old
  • PhD: Master's degree holder, under 40 years old
  • Good health
  • Good academic grades (minimum 12/20 for Bachelor's, 13/20 for Master's/PhD)

How to Apply?

  1. Choose a university and program
  2. Apply to the university (some require pre-admission)
  3. Apply for the CSC scholarship on studyinchina.csc.edu.cn
  4. Submit all required documents
  5. Wait for results (June-July)

For a detailed step-by-step guide, read our CSC Scholarship 2026 Complete Guide.

2026 Calendar

  • Opening: January 2026
  • Deadline: April 30, 2026 (varies by university)
  • Results: June - July 2026
  • Start of classes: September 2026

Universities Accepting CSC Scholarship

Over 270 Chinese universities accept the CSC scholarship, including:

  • Tsinghua University
  • Peking University
  • Fudan University
  • Zhejiang University
  • Shanghai Jiao Tong University
  • Wuhan University
  • And 260+ others...

Check the full list on the CSC website or on our scholarships page.

2. Provincial Scholarships (Local Governments)

Chinese provincial governments offer their own scholarships, often easier to obtain than the CSC.

Beijing Municipal Government Scholarship

  • Coverage: Tuition fees + monthly allowance
  • Allowance amount: 2,000-3,000 RMB/month
  • Universities: Universities in Beijing (Tsinghua, Peking, BUPT, etc.)
  • Deadline: April 2026

Shanghai Government Scholarship

  • Coverage: Tuition fees + monthly allowance
  • Allowance amount: 2,000-2,500 RMB/month
  • Universities: Fudan, SJTU, Tongji, etc.
  • Deadline: April 2026

Jiangsu Jasmine Scholarship

  • Coverage: Tuition fees + monthly allowance
  • Amount: 1,500-2,500 RMB/month
  • Universities: Nanjing University, Southeast University, etc.
  • Deadline: March-April 2026

Zhejiang Provincial Government Scholarship

  • Coverage: Tuition fees + allowance
  • Amount: 1,500-2,500 RMB/month
  • Universities: Zhejiang University, Zhejiang Normal University, etc.
  • Deadline: April 2026

Other Provincial Scholarships

  • Guangdong Government Scholarship
  • Liaoning Provincial Government Scholarship
  • Sichuan Provincial Government Scholarship
  • Hubei Provincial Government Scholarship

3. University Scholarships

Nearly ALL Chinese universities offer their own scholarships to international students.

Types of University Scholarships

Full Scholarships

  • 100% tuition + accommodation + monthly stipend
  • Similar to CSC but offered directly by the university
  • Competition: Medium to high

Partial Scholarships

  • Type 1: 75% tuition fee reduction
  • Type 2: 50% tuition fee reduction
  • Type 3: 25% tuition fee reduction

Academic Excellence Scholarships

  • Based on first-year grades
  • 1,000-5,000 RMB/semester for good results
  • Renewable each semester if good grades are maintained

Specific University Scholarship Examples

Tsinghua University Scholarship

  • Type A: Full coverage (tuition + accommodation + stipend)
  • Type B: Tuition + accommodation
  • Type C: Tuition only
  • Highly competitive (acceptance rate ~10-15%)

Peking University President's Scholarship

  • Full coverage
  • Stipend amount: 3,000-3,500 RMB/month
  • For exceptional students (GPA above 15/20)

Zhejiang University International Student Scholarship

  • 3 levels: Full, 75%, 50%
  • Moderate competition
  • Good option for students with 13-15/20 GPA

Wuhan University Scholarship

  • Full and partial scholarships available
  • High acceptance rate (~40-50% of applicants)
  • Excellent option to increase your chances

Strategy Tip: Always apply for university scholarships IN ADDITION to CSC and provincial scholarships. This multiplies your chances! See our guide on best universities for Moroccan students.

4. Confucius Institute Scholarships

Specifically designed for learning Chinese language and culture.

Types of Confucius Scholarships

4-Week Scholarship

  • Intensive Chinese language course (4 weeks)
  • Coverage: Tuition + accommodation + allowance
  • For beginners or intermediate learners

One-Semester Scholarship (5 months)

  • Intensive Chinese language program
  • Full coverage
  • Allowance: 2,500 RMB/month

One-Year Scholarship

  • Full year of Chinese language study
  • Full coverage + 2,500 RMB/month allowance
  • Perfect before starting a bachelor's program

Master's in Teaching Chinese (MTCSOL)

  • 2-year Master's program
  • Full coverage + 3,000 RMB/month allowance
  • For students with HSK 5-6

How to Apply?

  • Website: cis.chinese.cn
  • Deadline: Generally April-May 2026
  • Documents: Application form, passport, HSK certificate (if applicable), recommendation letters

5. Specialized Scholarships

Great Wall Program (PhD Only)

  • For PhD students in science, engineering, agriculture, medicine
  • Full coverage + 3,500 RMB/month allowance
  • Highly competitive

Silk Road Scholarship

  • For students from Silk Road countries (includes Morocco)
  • Bachelor's, Master's, PhD
  • Full coverage
  • Priority fields: business, economics, international relations

Huawei / Tech Company Scholarships

  • Offered by major Chinese companies
  • For students in computer science, engineering, AI
  • Often includes internship opportunities

Strategy to Maximize Your Chances

1. Apply to MULTIPLE Scholarships Simultaneously

Do not put all your eggs in one basket! Apply to:

  • CSC Scholarship (top priority)
  • Provincial scholarship for the city of your university
  • University scholarship
  • Confucius Institute (if interested in Chinese language)

Recommended total applications: 3-5 scholarships

2. Choose Universities Strategically

  • 1 TOP university: Tsinghua, Peking, Fudan (high competition, try your luck)
  • 1-2 MID-TIER universities: Wuhan, Nanjing, Xiamen (good chances)
  • 1 "SAFETY" university: Universities in tier-2/3 cities (very good chances)

See our detailed guide on best Chinese universities for Moroccan students.

3. Prepare an Excellent Application

Outstanding Motivation Letter:

  • Explain WHY China, WHY this university, WHY this program
  • Demonstrate your motivation and clear goals
  • 800-1,000 words, well-structured
  • No copy-pasting from generic templates!

Strong Recommendation Letters:

  • From professors who TRULY know you
  • Who can speak to your academic strengths
  • Personalized letter, not generic

Clean Transcripts:

  • Properly translated and notarized
  • Highlight your best grades

4. Improve Your Profile

  • English test: IELTS 6.0+ or TOEFL 80+ strengthens your application
  • HSK (Chinese): Even HSK 3-4 shows your interest in China
  • Extracurricular activities: Clubs, volunteering, competitions
  • Relevant experience: Internships, projects, publications

5. Respect ALL Deadlines

  • Start 6 months before deadlines
  • Submit 1-2 weeks BEFORE the deadline (not the last day!)
  • Keep a checklist of all required documents

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you combine multiple scholarships?

No. You can only accept ONE scholarship at a time. If you are accepted by multiple scholarship programs, you must choose one. However, applying to several simultaneously maximizes your chances of receiving at least one.

Which scholarship is the easiest to get?

University scholarships from tier-2/3 cities have the highest acceptance rates (40-60%). CSC and top-university scholarships (Tsinghua, Peking) are the most competitive (10-15% acceptance rate). Provincial scholarships are a good middle ground.

Do you need excellent grades to get a scholarship?

Not necessarily. With a GPA of 13-14/20 and a strong application (motivation letter, recommendation letters), you have good chances for university and provincial scholarships. A well-crafted dossier can compensate for average grades.

Do scholarships cover flight tickets?

Most scholarships do NOT cover the initial flight ticket. You will need to purchase it yourself (~$600-900 USD). However, all major scholarships cover tuition, accommodation, monthly stipend, and medical insurance.

Can you lose your scholarship during your studies?

Yes, if your grades fall below the minimum required level or if you violate university regulations. Scholarship recipients are reviewed annually. Maintain good academic performance and follow all university rules to keep your scholarship.
