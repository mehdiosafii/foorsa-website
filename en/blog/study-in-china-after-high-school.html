Study in China After High School: Complete Guide for Moroccan Bac Holders

Published on March 7, 2026 | By Foorsa Team

Just graduated with your Moroccan baccalaureate and dreaming of studying abroad? China is an outstanding destination for Moroccan bac holders: world-renowned universities, English-taught programs, affordable costs, and generous scholarships.

This complete guide covers everything you need to know about pursuing your higher education in China after the bac.

Why Choose China After High School?

1. World-Class Academic Quality

Chinese universities rank among the best in the world. Tsinghua, Peking University, Fudan, and Zhejiang University compete directly with top American and European institutions. Explore the best Chinese universities for Moroccan students.

2. English-Taught Programs

Hundreds of bachelor's programs are taught entirely in English. You do not need to speak Chinese to begin your studies, although learning Mandarin is a valuable bonus.

3. Affordable Costs

Studying in China costs 50-70% less than in Europe or North America, while offering comparable academic quality. See our full fees and financing breakdown.

4. Abundant Scholarships

The Chinese government and universities offer thousands of scholarships every year. Many Moroccan bac holders study in China completely FREE through scholarships. Learn about the CSC Scholarship 2026.

5. Unique International Experience

Living in China opens the doors to Asia, allows you to learn a strategically important language (Mandarin), and gives you a tremendous competitive advantage in the job market.

Available Programs After the Bac in China

Bachelor's Degree Programs -- 4 Years

China offers bachelor's programs in virtually every field:

  • Engineering & Technology: Civil engineering, computer science, mechanical, electrical, aerospace
  • Medicine: MBBS (6 years), pharmacy, traditional Chinese medicine
  • Business & Economics: International trade, finance, management
  • Sciences: Physics, chemistry, biology, mathematics
  • Languages & Communication: Chinese, English, journalism
  • Arts & Design: Architecture, graphic design, fine arts

Browse our majors page to explore all available options.

MBBS (Medicine) -- 6 Years

China is one of the most popular destinations for international medical students. The MBBS program is taught in English at WHO-listed universities and is recognized internationally. Read our detailed guide on studying medicine in China.

Chinese Language Foundation Year (Optional)

If your program is taught in Chinese, or if you simply want to learn the language, you can complete a 1-year Chinese language preparatory program before starting your bachelor's degree.

Admission Requirements for Moroccan Bac Holders

Academic Requirements

  • Diploma: Moroccan Baccalaureate (all series accepted)
  • Minimum grades: Generally 12/20 or higher (varies by university)
  • Key subjects: Depends on the major (math/physics for engineering, sciences for medicine, etc.)

Language Requirements

For English-taught programs:

  • IELTS 5.5+ or TOEFL 65+ (some universities waive this for students who studied in English)
  • University's own English proficiency test (possible alternative)

For Chinese-taught programs:

  • HSK 4 minimum (or complete a Chinese language preparatory year)

Age Requirements

  • Maximum 25 years old for bachelor's programs
  • No minimum age (typically 17-18 after the bac)

Required Documents

  • Completed application form
  • Passport copy
  • Baccalaureate diploma (notarized and translated)
  • High school transcripts (notarized and translated)
  • Language proficiency certificate (English or Chinese)
  • Motivation letter / Study plan (800-1,000 words)
  • Two recommendation letters (from high school teachers)
  • Medical certificate
  • Clean criminal record
  • Passport-size photos

For document preparation help, see our documents guide.

How Much Does It Cost to Study in China?

Tuition Fees (Per Year)

  • Arts & Humanities: 2,500 - 4,500 USD/year
  • Business & Economics: 3,000 - 5,500 USD/year
  • Science & Engineering: 3,500 - 6,000 USD/year
  • Medicine (MBBS): 4,500 - 8,000 USD/year

Total tuition for 4 years: 10,000 - 32,000 USD

Living Costs (Per Year)

  • Accommodation (university dormitory): 400 - 1,500 USD/year
  • Food: 1,500 - 2,500 USD/year
  • Local transport: 200 - 400 USD/year
  • Books & supplies: 200 - 400 USD/year
  • Health insurance (mandatory): 100 - 150 USD/year
  • Personal expenses: 800 - 1,500 USD/year

Total living costs: 3,200 - 6,500 USD/year (depending on the city)

Total Cost for a 4-Year Bachelor's Degree

  • Budget estimate: ~18,000 USD (~52,000 MAD/year)
  • Average estimate: ~30,000 USD (~87,000 MAD/year)
  • Higher-end estimate: ~50,000 USD (~145,000 MAD/year)

See our detailed breakdown on fees and financing.

Scholarships Available for Moroccan Bac Holders

1. Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)

The most prestigious and comprehensive scholarship:

  • Coverage: 100% tuition + accommodation + monthly allowance (2,500 RMB) + insurance
  • Duration: Full program length (4 years + 1 year of Chinese language if needed)
  • Application window: January - April 2026
  • Results: June - July 2026

Learn how to apply in our CSC Scholarship 2026 guide.

2. University Scholarships

Each Chinese university offers its own scholarships:

  • Partial scholarships: 25-75% tuition reduction
  • Full scholarships: Similar to CSC (tuition + accommodation + allowance)
  • Process: Apply directly to the university

3. Provincial Government Scholarships

Provincial governments (Beijing, Shanghai, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, etc.) also offer scholarships to international students with varying levels of coverage.

4. Confucius Institute Scholarships

Specific scholarships for studying Chinese language and culture, available through Confucius Institutes worldwide.

Pro tip: To maximize your chances, apply for multiple scholarships simultaneously! Visit our scholarships page for the full list.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Research & Selection (December - February)

  • Explore universities and programs
  • Check admission requirements
  • Identify available scholarships
  • Contact universities for information

Step 2: Document Preparation (January - March)

  • Translate and notarize your academic documents
  • Take the English proficiency test if required
  • Write your motivation letter / study plan
  • Obtain recommendation letters
  • Complete the medical examination

Step 3: Submit Applications (March - May)

  • Apply through university online portals
  • Apply for the CSC scholarship (if eligible)
  • Pay application fees (typically 50-150 USD)
  • Track your application status

Step 4: Receive Results (June - July)

  • Admission Notice (acceptance letter)
  • JW202 Form (visa document)
  • Scholarship notification (if applicable)

Step 5: Visa Application (July - August)

  • Apply for the X1 student visa at the Chinese Embassy in Morocco
  • Required documents: passport, admission notice, JW202, medical examination
  • Processing time: 4-7 business days
  • Cost: approximately 600 MAD

Step 6: Departure & Arrival (August - September)

  • Book your flight
  • Arrive in China (universities often arrange airport pickup)
  • Register at the university
  • Apply for your residence permit
  • Move into the dormitory

Timeline for September 2026 Intake

  • December 2025 - February 2026: Research & document preparation
  • March - April 2026: Submit applications & scholarship forms
  • May - June 2026: Wait for results
  • July - August 2026: Visa application & departure preparation
  • September 2026: Arrive in China & begin classes

Important: Top universities have limited spots and early deadlines (end of March). Start your preparations now!

Student Life in China

Accommodation

Most international students live in university dormitories (400-1,500 USD/year). Rooms are typically double or single with private bathroom, internet access, and sometimes a shared kitchen.

Food

University canteens are very affordable (2-4 USD per meal). You will also find Chinese and international restaurants near campus.

Transportation

Chinese cities have excellent public transportation: metro, buses, shared bikes. Monthly cost: 15-30 USD with a student card.

Language

Even if you study in English, learning basic Chinese makes daily life much easier. Universities offer free Chinese language courses for international students.

Activities

Student clubs, sports, cultural events, travel opportunities... Student life in China is rich and dynamic.

Tips for a Successful Application

1. Start Early

Do not wait until you have your bac results. Begin preparing your application file in December-January of your final year.

2. Apply to Multiple Universities

Target 2-3 universities: one top-tier (Tsinghua, Peking), one mid-range, and one "safety" option.

3. Write an Excellent Motivation Letter

Clearly explain why you want to study in China, why this specific program, and your career goals.

4. Get Strong Recommendation Letters

Ask your best teachers (math, physics, English -- depending on your chosen field).

5. Improve Your English

A strong IELTS/TOEFL score strengthens your application and makes the transition easier.

6. Get Professional Guidance

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I study in China after my Moroccan bac?

Yes, Moroccan bac holders can apply directly to Chinese universities for bachelor's programs. All bac series are accepted, though most universities require a minimum grade of 12/20. Many programs are taught entirely in English.

How much does it cost to study in China?

Tuition ranges from 2,500 to 8,000 USD/year depending on the field. Living costs are approximately 3,200-6,500 USD/year. The total cost for a 4-year bachelor's is roughly 18,000-50,000 USD -- 50-70% cheaper than Europe or North America.

What scholarships are available?

The main scholarships include: CSC (Chinese Government Scholarship) covering 100% of costs, university scholarships (25-100% tuition), provincial scholarships, and Confucius Institute scholarships. Many Moroccan students study in China completely free. See our scholarships page.

Do I need to speak Chinese?

No, hundreds of programs are taught in English. You need IELTS 5.5+ or TOEFL 65+ for English-taught programs. For Chinese-taught programs, HSK 4 is required, or you can complete a preparatory language year first.

What is the application deadline for September 2026?

Most applications are due between March and May 2026. CSC scholarship applications open January-April 2026. Top universities have early deadlines (end of March), so start preparing now.

Can I study medicine in China after my bac?

Yes, China offers 6-year MBBS programs taught in English at WHO-listed universities. Moroccan bac holders with strong science grades can apply directly. Read our guide on studying medicine in China.
