About the CSCA Exam

What is the CSCA?

The China Scholastic Competency Assessment (CSCA) is a standardized entrance exam for international students applying to Chinese universities. Starting from 2026, it is required by 306+ universities for undergraduate admissions, including CSC scholarship applicants.

Real Exam Format

  • Mathematics — 48 MCQs, 60 minutes (compulsory for all)
  • Physics — 48 MCQs, 60 minutes (optional, for STEM programs)
  • Chemistry — 48 MCQs, 60 minutes (optional, for STEM/Medicine)
  • Professional Chinese — 80 MCQs, 90 minutes (for Chinese-taught programs)
  • Each subject scored out of 100 points
  • Held 5 times per year: January, March, April, June, December
  • No calculators allowed

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the CSCA exam?

The CSCA (China Scholastic Competency Assessment) is a standardized entrance exam launched in 2025 for international students applying to Chinese universities. It covers Mathematics (compulsory), Physics, Chemistry, and Professional Chinese. Starting 2026, it is required by 306+ universities.

What subjects does the real CSCA test?

The CSCA has 4 subjects: Mathematics (compulsory for all), Physics (optional), Chemistry (optional), and Professional Chinese (for Chinese-taught programs). Each foundation subject has 48 MCQs in 60 minutes. Our simulator covers Math, Physics, and Chemistry.

Is this simulator free?

Yes! Foorsa provides this CSCA simulator completely free — no registration, no limits. Practice with timed exams or study mode as many times as you want.

When is the next CSCA exam?

The CSCA is held 5 times per year: January, March, April, June, and December. Registration is on the official CSCA website (csca.cn). The exam fee is 450 RMB for one subject or 700 RMB for two or more.
